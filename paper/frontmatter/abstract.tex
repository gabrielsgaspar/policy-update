\begin{abstract}
This paper asks whether party-owned digital platforms can transmit citizen voice into party agenda and policy behavior. The empirical setting is Beppe Grillo's blog and the emergence of the Five Star Movement (M5S) in Italy. The planned analysis links supporter comments, Grillo/M5S posts, party programs, parliamentary outputs, and external agenda controls to test whether online issue attention and policy proposals predict later party communication and formal political activity. This scaffold records the intended design only. Data collection, measurement validation, empirical estimates, and results are TBD.
\end{abstract}
