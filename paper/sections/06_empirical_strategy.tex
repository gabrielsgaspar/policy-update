\section{Empirical Strategy}\label{sec:empirical-strategy}

The planned empirical strategy begins with an issue-week panel. The key test is whether comment issue attention predicts future M5S issue attention after controlling for prior party attention, media and public agenda controls, comparison-party attention, elections, events, issue fixed effects, and time fixed effects.

A second design follows extracted policy proposals over time and tests whether proposal features predict candidate adoption in later party text or formal political outputs. Candidate features include repetition by distinct commenters, specificity, local information, evidence use, stance, novelty, and movement phase.

The current language discipline is predictive and associational. A causal interpretation is TBD and requires stronger evidence on timing, common shocks, archive missingness, and placebo tests. See \texttt{docs/IDENTIFICATION.md}.
