# Research Project

Last updated: 2026-05-14

## One-sentence pitch

This project studies whether Beppe Grillo's blog and related Five Star Movement (M5S) digital spaces converted online supporter voice into party agenda, policy language, campaign materials, and formal political outputs.

## Research question

When citizens use a party-owned digital platform to express grievances, priorities, local information, and policy proposals, does the party learn from them?

## Motivation

Digital parties often claim to democratize politics by lowering the cost of participation. The central democratic question is whether online voice changes what parties do, or whether participation mainly mobilizes and legitimates supporters while leaders retain control.

## Core claim

TBD. The first paper should test whether digital parties can act as low-cost learning organizations, whether they mainly operate as mobilization machines, or whether responsiveness is selective and changes with institutionalization.

## Contribution

The project aims to link citizen input, party communication, formal political outputs, and external agenda controls in a longitudinal text network for the M5S case. Its contribution depends on validating whether comment-derived issue attention and proposals can be recovered and linked to later outputs.

## Mechanism

Supporter comments may provide high-frequency information about issue priorities, local grievances, policy proposals, and mobilization opportunities. M5S may respond by shifting attention, adopting language, elevating proposals, or translating themes into programs and parliamentary behavior. Responsiveness may vary by issue, proposal specificity, comment repetition, local information, movement phase, and institutional constraints.

## Empirical setting

Italy, Beppe Grillo's blog, Il Blog delle Stelle, M5S official digital spaces, and M5S formal political outputs. The intended main period is 2005-2018, with a first pilot focused on 2005-2013.

## Data

See `DATA.md` and `docs/DATA_SOURCES.md`.

Expected source groups are:

- Beppe Grillo blog posts and comments.
- Il Blog delle Stelle pages and comment threads.
- Wayback CDX captures and archived HTML/WARC files.
- Internet Archive item `BeppeGrillo.it2005`.
- M5S programs, campaign materials, platform outputs, and official documents.
- Chamber of Deputies, Senate, and Openpolis/OpenParlamento outputs.
- Election results from Italian official sources.
- Media and public agenda controls, including CAP/Italian Policy Agendas, Manifesto Project, GDELT, Google Trends, and AGCOM if feasible.
- Comparison-party programs, websites, and parliamentary outputs.
- A curated political/event calendar.

## Identification strategy

See `docs/IDENTIFICATION.md`.

Current design is best described as predictive/associational until the data and timing structure are validated. The main candidate designs are issue-week lead-lag panels, proposal-level event history, placebo tests, comparison-party outcomes, and institutionalization-break heterogeneity.

## Main results so far

None. The repository is organized, but no data collection or analysis has been executed.

## Current bottleneck

Historical comment recoverability and completeness: whether enough comment threads, dates/order, pagination, and archival coverage can be recovered to support valid measurement.
