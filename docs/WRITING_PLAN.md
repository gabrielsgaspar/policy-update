# Writing Plan

## Target Paper Structure

The default LaTeX structure is:

1. Introduction
2. Related literature
3. Context
4. Theory
5. Data
6. Empirical strategy
7. Results
8. Robustness
9. Conclusion

This structure is suitable for the project, but results and robustness sections should remain placeholders until analysis exists.

5. Contribution: digital representation, party responsiveness, internet/media political economy, M5S/digital parties, and validated text-as-data methods.

## Style Rules

- Be explicit about whether claims are descriptive, predictive, associational, or causal.
- Do not overstate responsiveness before analysis.
- Do not describe recoverability as known until source inventory is complete.
- Put the contribution early.
- Tie literature paragraphs to the project's marginal contribution.
- Avoid placeholder citations in final prose.
- Use project-specific prose rather than generic scaffold language.

## Required Before Drafting Results

- feasibility memo;
- source inventory;
- archive completeness metrics;
- validation plan and initial label audit;
- PI decision on first-paper sample and claim language.
